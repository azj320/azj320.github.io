Responsive Redesign

Oct 18, 2022

Pick a Web Page

The website I choose to re-design is Yakima Theater's official website. Yakima Theater is a family owned theater in Washington. The theater has been there since 1912 and made it through the pandemic. It's my favourite movie theater whenever I visited the area. They have great staffs and nice environment but their website is ugly and poorly designed. I believe their business will be much better if they improve their website. Link to the web

Finding Problems

Visual
  • Their logo on the top takes too much space. Users have to scroll down to view their content.
  • There is no padding around the pick a location button.
  • There is no line or color to define each section.
  • The facebook logo and sign in button are placed in unconventional positions. Users might need time to find them.
  • The yellow color doesn't have strong contrast with the blue color or the white color. It's hard to see the words.
  • The info on the right hand side takes too much space.
Usability, Learnability and etc
  • There are three different locations for the Yakima Theater but each has a different name. It's confusing for a first time user what they are.
  • Users can only choose two of the three locations under the button "Please Choose Location". The other location has its own website, and it's on the right hand side of the page.
  • It's not clear what time the movie ends.
  • The choose Location button is too close to the Showtimes button which makes it hard to click.
  • The state of the Location button is not up-to-date when choosing the location through the Showtimes button.
  • It's not clear what the Search algorithm is based on.
  • The purpose of the section Theater Info is not clear. Users are expecting to find locations and picture of the theater, but they only have the rules on the page.

Accessibility

    I agree to most of its findings. I found some same errors in the previous section. WAVE also helps me find some errors or warnings I didn't notice earlier.

  • Missing first level heading on their home page
  • Redundant link in email sign-up section

    • There is one error that don't really show on the page but in their code

  • The empty link is an error in their code but not shown on the page. There is no button or text that allows user to click on the empty link on their website.
